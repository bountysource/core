Fees & Payments

Fundraiser fees

Creating a Fundraiser

We do not charge anything to create a Fundraiser. Go create one now!

Flexible Funding vs. All-or-nothing

By default, all Fundraisers utilize a “Flexible Funding” model. This means that a developer will receive all money pledged to the Fundraiser even if the goal is not met.

However, developers can opt-in to an “All-or-nothing” campaign. In this model, a developer only receives the money pledged if the goal is met. Otherwise, all backers are refunded.

Read “When should I choose All-or-nothing?” in our FAQ for more information.

Fundraiser payout

A 10% fee is deducted from the amount raised. This fee includes the payment processing fees incurred for collecting pledges. There are no other fees or charges from Bountysource.

Backing a Fundraiser

You will be charged the exact amount you pledge the moment you make your pledge.

In the case of an “All-or-nothing” Fundraiser that fails to hit its goal, you will be refunded the pledged amount less a 10% fee.


Bounty fees

Claiming a bounty

We do not charge anything to claim a bounty. The amount displayed as the bounty total is the exact amount a developer will receive upon payout.

Placing a bounty

Bountysource charges a 10% non-refundable fee for placing bounties in addition to the specified bounty amount.

For example, to place a $1,000 bounty on an issue, you will be charged $1,100.


Payments

“Payout” vs. “Cashout”

A Payout is a transfer of money from Bountysource to a Bountysource account.

A Cashout is a withdrawal of your Bountysource balance. All cashouts are processed on a per-request basis. We can send you money via Paypal, Google Wallet, physical check or bank wire. Bountysource is not responsible for any fees incurred due to the chosen cashout method.

To request a cashout of your current Bountysource balance, please use contact us. Cashouts happen on the Friday following a request.

Bounties

Bounties are automatically paid out to a developer’s Bountysource account once their solution is accepted and merged.
